Mindset Matters

Before we dive into understanding your multilingual learners, let's examine two different ways of thinking about these students. The words we use matter. Our expectations matter. And our beliefs about student potential become self-fulfilling prophecies.

Deficit Mindset

  • "Limited English proficiency"
  • "Behind in reading"
  • "Parent doesn't participate"
  • "Needs remedial support"
  • "Language barrier prevents learning"

Teacher Responses:

  • Lower expectations
  • Simplified tasks
  • Exclusion from advanced work
  • Focus on deficits

Asset Mindset

  • "Developing multilingual abilities"
  • "Strong mathematical reasoning"
  • "Family prioritizes education highly"
  • "Brings rich cultural knowledge"
  • "Developing academic English while demonstrating comprehension"

Teacher Responses:

  • High expectations with supports
  • Multilingual resources
  • Family partnership strategies
  • Focus on strengths
